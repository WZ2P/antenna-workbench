2 · Antenna Type

Higher τ / σ = more gain, more elements, longer boom. Carrel curves: τ 0.88, σ 0.05 ≈ 6.5 dBi.

Each added director buys ~1 dB and a longer boom. Past 4 elements the returns get thin — the boom doesn't.

Ladder-line insulation slows the wave (velocity factor), so every dimension shrinks. Copper-clad steel conducts like copper at RF (skin effect) — it's the coating that sets the length, not the steel. VFs per M0UKD; solid-ribbon 300 Ω varies (0.85–0.91), so cut long and trim.

Ground-mounted: the height field is ignored for the pattern (base sits at ground). Elevated: the feed-point height drives both the match and the pattern.

Core Mixes & The Derating Math

Mix #31The choking champion 160–40 m; good through HF. First pick for 1:1 common-mode chokes.
Mix #43The general-purpose HF transformer mix — 9:1, 49:1, 64:1 ununs and 4:1 baluns live here.
Mix #52Lower loss at HF than #43 — the high-power transformer upgrade before you resort to big stacks.
Mix #61VHF work (50 MHz +). Wrong tool below 20 m.
Powdered iron (T-series)For tuned circuits and inductors — NOT for these broadband transformers.
Safe dissipationFT-140 ≈ 8 W · FT-240 ≈ 16 W continuous, free air. Stacking n cores ≈ n× dissipation AND n× power capability.
The mathHeat = PPEP × duty × 50 % tx-time × loss-fraction → cores = ⌈heat ÷ per-core dissipation⌉
Voltage vs heatInsulation and capacitors are stressed by PEP (instantaneous volts); cores are cooked by average power (duty). Derate each by its own physics.
